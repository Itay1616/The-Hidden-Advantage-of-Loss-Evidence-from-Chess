# The-Hidden-Advantage-of-Loss-Evidence-from-Chess
This study leverages chess games from various tournaments to investigate the hot hand. Unlike most previous studies, our approach utilizes a natural data environment. Using this comprehensive dataset, we examine how prior game outcomes affect participants' subsequent success, controlling for player ability and piece color. Our focus is on analyzing the hot hand effect between games, particularly how success or failure influences future performance and the role of the opponent's previous outcome. Our findings reveal no evidence of the hot hand phenomenon. Moreover, we uncover a surprising result: after a player wins and subsequently faces an opponent who lost their previous game, the player's average score drop by about 6%. This effect holds across skill levels, affecting both expert and amateur players
